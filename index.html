A Brief History

How We Found Each Other

✦

Three days, told twice. The same hours, remembered from two sides of the room.

Chapter the First

The First Encounter

The day we noticed each other
As she tells it

Her side.

I wasn't looking for him. I was looking for my keys, or my coffee, or some small thing that had gone missing — and then there he was, mid-sentence with someone else, and I lost the thread of whatever I'd been doing.

I told myself I didn't care if he looked over. He looked over.

As he tells it

His side.

I saw her across the room and forgot what I was saying. Whoever I was talking to deserves an apology — I have no memory of the rest of that conversation.

I went home that night and told a friend I had met the woman I was going to marry. He laughed. I didn't.

✦
Chapter the Second

The First Date

A long dinner, and a longer walk after
As she tells it

Her side.

I changed three times before I left the house and arrived five minutes late on purpose. He was already there, of course, reading the menu like it was a test he intended to pass.

We closed the restaurant. Then we walked, and walked, and walked. I remember the sound of the city more than anything either of us said.

As he tells it

His side.

I got there twenty minutes early and pretended I'd just arrived. I rehearsed three things to say and used none of them.

She laughed at something I didn't mean to be funny, and that was the end of any plan I had. We walked until the streetlights started going out.

✦
Chapter the Third

The Proposal

A porch in Colorado, the slow hour before dinner
As she tells it

Her side.

He had been quiet all afternoon, which is not like him, and I had decided he was hungry. The light on the porch was that gold that only happens for about eleven minutes a day, and then he was on one knee, and my hands forgot what they were for.

The answer had been yes for a long time. I just got to finally say it out loud.

As he tells it

His side.

I had the ring in my pocket for three days, waiting for a moment that felt right. I kept moving it from one pocket to another like that would help.

The porch, the light, her — I stopped waiting. I asked. She said yes before I'd quite finished the question, which is the kindest thing anyone has ever done for me.
